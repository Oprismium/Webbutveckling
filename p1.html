The Kingdom of Sweden

A Chronicle of Crown, Steel, and North

Origins

Sweden emerged from the Norse world during the Viking Age, shaped by trade, warfare, and the slow consolidation of royal power.

The Medieval Kingdom

Christianity, law codes, and monarchy bound the land into a realm, culminating in the Kalmar Union and centuries of struggle for independence.

The Swedish Empire

In the 17th century, Sweden rose as a great power under kings like Gustavus Adolphus, dominating the Baltic through discipline and fire.

Modern Sweden

Defeat, reform, and neutrality reshaped Sweden into a constitutional monarchy known today for stability, industry, and diplomacy.
